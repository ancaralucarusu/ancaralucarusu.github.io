Course overview

What this course teaches

Designed for professionals who support peers in their career journeys, this course builds practical coaching competencies grounded in ICF coaching principles, positive psychology, and structured decision-making frameworks.

01
Foundations of Peer Career Coaching
Introduces the coaching mindset — curiosity, non-judgment, and non-directiveness. Distinguishes coaching from mentoring, advising, and training through interactive sorting activities. Covers active listening and presence, including the three levels of listening.
Coaching vs Mentoring Active Listening Coaching Mindset Trust Building
02
Expanding Awareness: Exploration and Powerful Questions
Teaches how to formulate open, powerful questions that expand awareness. Introduces the Job Satisfaction Wheel as a practical exploration tool. Covers the Awareness Expansion Process and supportive feedback techniques through real-world scenarios.
Powerful Questions Job Satisfaction Wheel Perspective Expansion Feedback
03
Clarifying Direction: Values, Strengths, and Goal Setting
Guides learners through the Direction-Setting Sequence — clarifying values, identifying strengths, and exploring interests. Uses a Venn Diagram to synthesise themes, then supports SMART goal setting with alignment between values and goals.
Values Discovery Strengths Identification SMART Goals Direction Setting
04
Decisions and Design: Choosing and Shaping the Path
Introduces structured career decision-making — from decision matrices to dilemma work. Explores Job Crafting as a tool for shaping roles, and co-creating actionable, accountable plans with a 5-step Action Planning framework.
Decision Matrix Job Crafting Action Planning Accountability
05
Action, Feedback, and Growth: Supporting Progress and Reflection
Focuses on sustaining growth through coaching-oriented feedback, accountability structures, and meaningful reflection. Uses flashcard activities, real-world scenarios, and Alex's continuous case study to bring concepts to life.
Growth Feedback Accountability Reflection Identity Development

Design decisions

The instructional design thinking behind this course

Every design choice in this course was deliberate — grounded in learning psychology and built to close the gap between knowledge and real-world coaching practice.

🧵

Continuous Case Study (Alex)

A single protagonist — Alex — runs through all 5 modules. This narrative thread reduces cognitive load, builds emotional investment, and lets learners see how concepts connect across the full coaching journey, not just in isolation.

🔄

Scenario-Based Practice

Each module includes interactive scenarios where learners apply skills to real situations before being given feedback. This retrieval-practice approach — grounded in cognitive science — deepens retention far beyond passive reading.

🧠

Psychology-Informed Sequencing

The module order follows the natural arc of a coaching engagement: from mindset → awareness → direction → decision → growth. This mirrors real practice, making transfer to behaviour much more likely than a topic-by-topic structure.

🎯

Practical Frameworks as Anchors

The Job Satisfaction Wheel, Decision Matrix, and SMART goal framework give learners concrete tools they can use immediately — turning abstract coaching theory into tangible, repeatable practice.

✅

Formative Assessment Throughout

Knowledge checks are woven in at the end of every section — not just at the end of each module. This spaced retrieval approach strengthens long-term retention and gives learners immediate, low-stakes feedback on their understanding.

🌱

Identity-Centred Reflection

The final module goes beyond skills to address identity — helping learners see themselves as coaches. Research shows that professional identity is a stronger predictor of sustained behaviour change than knowledge or skill alone.

Instructional design competencies demonstrated

This course is evidence of a specific set of instructional design capabilities — here is how they map to what you see in the course.

✓

Needs analysis applied: Course is tightly scoped to a real, specific audience — peer supporters — with learning objectives mapped directly to what they need to do, not just know.

✓

Backward design: Every module starts from the behaviour change outcome and works backward — the Alex case study exists specifically to bridge the knowing-doing gap.

✓

Learning psychology in practice: Cognitive load managed through progressive disclosure; spaced retrieval through distributed knowledge checks; motivation maintained through narrative continuity.

✓

Articulate Rise proficiency: Effective use of Rise blocks — flashcards, scenarios, sorting activities, Venn diagrams, and knowledge checks — chosen for pedagogical fit, not decoration.

✓

SME depth: The content reflects genuine expertise in ICF coaching methodology, positive psychology, and career development frameworks — not surface-level summaries.

✓

Learner-centred tone: Language is warm, direct, and respectful throughout — written for adult professionals, not generic corporate audiences.
