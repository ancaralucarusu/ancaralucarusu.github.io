The difference

Why hire a designer
with my background?

Most instructional designers come from education or technology. I come from a third place — the human side of learning.

01

Psychology first. Science always.

With a BA and MSc in Psychology, I design with cognitive load theory, spaced repetition, and behaviour change built in — not added as an afterthought. I don’t just know what engaging looks like; I know exactly why it works.

02

I’ve lived every role in the room.

As an L&D Specialist, HR Business Partner, Career Counsellor, and Scrum Master, I’ve been the stakeholder, the facilitator, the analyst, and the learner. I know what managers actually need and what learners quietly resist.

03

Coaching intelligence in every module.

As an ICF-PCC certified coach, I understand the mechanics of reflection, habit formation, and sustainable change. I design these moments directly into courses — so learning transfers to real behaviour, not just test scores.

04

UX thinking. Learner-centred always.

Google UX Design certified (2023). I approach every course the way a product designer approaches an interface — with empathy mapping, iterative prototyping, and the learner’s experience driving every decision.

05

Fluent in complexity and context.

IT companies, universities, NGOs, international coaching clients — I’ve designed for radically different audiences. Great e-learning is never generic; it’s always built for specific people with specific needs.
