The Difference

Why hire an e-learning
designer with my background?

Most instructional designers come from one of two places: either education/content, or technology. I come from a third place — the human side. Here's what that means for the learning experiences I create.

🧠

Psychology first. Learning science always.

With a BA and MSc in Psychology, I design with cognitive load theory, motivation, spaced repetition, and behaviour change baked in — not bolted on. I don't just know what engaging looks like, I know why it works.

🎯

I've been the learner, the facilitator, and the stakeholder.

Having designed L&D programmes, facilitated workshops, conducted needs analyses, and coached individuals — I understand every angle of a learning challenge. I know what managers need, what learners resist, and what makes content land.

💬

Coaching intelligence. Built into every module.

As an ICF-PCC certified coach, I know how people reflect, build new habits, and get stuck. I use this to design reflection moments, self-assessment activities, and content that actually changes behaviour — not just knowledge scores.

🎨

UX thinking. Learner-centred by design.

Google UX Design certified (2023), I approach course design with the same empathy-driven, iterative thinking as product design — putting the learner experience at the centre of every decision.

🌍

Real-world context. Across sectors and cultures.

IT companies, universities, NGOs, international clients — I've worked across environments where one-size-fits-all never fits. I design e-learning that is contextually sensitive, accessible, and genuinely useful for the audience it's built for.
